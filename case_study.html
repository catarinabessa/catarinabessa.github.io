description

in anniversary night, the player assumes the paper of paul, a gay cop who struggles with derogatory comments and threats in his workplace.
in this day, it is paul and his husband's anniversary but unfortunately this night doesn't go as it should: the couple and their daughter go through an awful and unexpected ordeal and it's up to the player to make what they deem the right choices.

specifics

anniversary night is a pixel art videogame demo with an interactive narrative and includes multiple choices. it was developed from march 2024 to june 2024.

problem and objective

the role of a police officer in games is often portrayed negatively. But what if there was a game about an honest police officer? what if he was dealing with family issues because of being a “hostage” to his job?
few cases in this medium explore the dangers in a police officer's life and their mental and physical consequences. what would be the impact of a tragic night on a police officer's mind?
currently, there are many games where the main character is part of the lgbtqia+ community; however, they often focus on a teenager questioning, "who am i?". what if the police officer was part of the lgbtqia+ community? how would that affect his life and his relationships with colleagues, both on and off duty?
all these questions, and many more, gave life to this project: an interactive narrative game about a gay police officer going through a hellish night with his husband and daughter. the player's choices will determine the protagonist's and his family's fate.
this project englobes homophobic themes in the workplace; being a hostage of their own job and how that affects their relationships; and the consequences that the dangerous job of being a policeman bring to the mind. continuous and intensive investigation about these topics were made in order to craft a realistic and compelling story as well as a relatable protagonist.
brooklyn nine-nine is an american comedy series. the story follows seven nypd (new york police department) detectives and their commander, raymond holt.
in the series, raymond is portrayed as the nypd's first black and openly gay police commander. because of these realities, raymond faced discrimination and marginalization in his field, especially when he began his career in the 1980s.
it was a constant struggle to be taken seriously, and he spent years without being promoted, despite being the most competent officer among his peers.

game references

'until dawn', supermassive games, 2015

this game serves as the foundation for the gameplay of anniversary night. much of the gameplay revolves around multiple choices that shape the story's progression, as well as quick-time events scattered throughout the game, which can also determine whether the character the player is controlling at the moment survives or dies.
to create a higher level of tension, both until dawn and anniversary night incorporate timers for choices and quick-time events. these elements make the game more unsettling and realistic. players must make rapid decisions that steer the story in completely different directions. 'undertale', toby fox, 2015

in undertale, each character has a unique sound—a pattern made up of “beeps” and “boops” that reflect their personality. whenever the player interacts with the inhabitants of the underground, these sounds can be heard, varying in rhythm, tone, and duration. this sound design choice was crucial in shaping the audio profile of the characters in anniversary night.
'bloomtown: a different story',
lazy bear games & different sense games, unreleased

similar to bloomtown: a different story, anniversary night uses a black background to surround the environments, focusing the player's attention on the main action. this approach reduces distractions from excessive assets or animations and prevents the player from being overwhelmed by overly detailed surroundings.
additionally, instead of using a text box at the bottom of the screen for character dialogue, anniversary night features speech bubbles appearing above the speaking character. unlike text boxes, which could obscure animations and take up nearly half the screen, speech bubbles are far less intrusive and feel more natural. 'the darkside detective', spooky doorway, 2017

the minimalist design of the characters, represented by the use of few pixels and faceless designs, served as an important reference in developing the characters for anniversary night.

game concept

anniversary night is an interactive narrative game where the player assumes the role of paul, a gay police officer who faces homophobic comments and insults at work.
no formal introduction is provided. as soon as the game begins, the player is "dropped" directly into a homophobic conversation between two police officers about the character they control. this approach aims to enhance the immersion in the protagonist's world, making the player forget they are playing a video game.
using a black screen with an introduction explaining the character, their family relationships, and workplace struggles could break this immersion, preventing the player from fully inhabiting the world as if they were the protagonist themselves.
throughout the story, the player will make various choices that affect the protagonist's career, family relationships, and the lives of all three family members.
during these moments, a timer will be implemented to increase the pressure of decision-making and create tension. the player is forced to react quickly, without overthinking. this mechanic makes these pivotal moments feel closer to real-life scenarios. for instance, if someone points a gun at another person, threatening to shoot, there are only seconds to act before it's too late.
as the story progresses, the player will gain insight into how homophobia remains pervasive in the 21st century, especially in police departments, as well as the risks and deeply negative psychological consequences of such a career.
by the end, the player will see how their actions impacted the different members of the family. learning how their decisions influenced each character's life is meant to weigh on the player's conscience, motivating them to try their luck and play again.

game development

we developed a flowchart which englobed the story and every action that the player may take and its consequences.

In total, there are seven characters in the game:
paul, the police officer consumed by his work.
raj, a tv chef with a complicated relationship with alcohol and paul's husband.
ollie, their adopted daughter, who is extremely energetic and currently obsessed with dinosaurs.
bigodes, a representation of an old-school cop, a “sheriff” type who speaks his mind without filter.
chad (jackson), a stereotype of what it means to "be a man" or "be macho," resembling a johnny bravo figure.
an organized and intelligent intruder, as stylish as she is malicious.
a robber, who attempts to rob a drugstore (this character does not appear in the game due to cuts of the initial plan).

anniversary night was made in unity. in total 5 scripts in c# were created. these were the following: the main menu, the settings menu, the controller, the choices script and the text script.

the original sounds were all created on audacity while the rest, were reappropriation of sounds already existent that then suffered alterations.
the combination of realistic sounds and stylized dialogue creates an interesting auditory experience due to its contrast. on one hand, immersion and realism are present in the atmospheric sound design; on the other, the characters' “beeps” and “boops” add a more playful aspect to the audio experience. all of this enhances the gameplay and enriches the player's overall experience.

final work

conclusion

throughout the development of this project, we deepened our understanding of the topics we initially aimed to address, thanks to extensive research and investigation.
the real-life experiences of gay individuals helped us grasp the nature of discrimination, both past and present, in workplaces and daily life. we also came to better understand the various consequences of being a police officer, from the dangers and stress inherent to the profession to the impacts on mental health and the concerns for their loved ones.
this work provided a positive experience, fostering group unity and offering new learning opportunities for each member, ranging from programming (as no group member had prior programming knowledge) to the creation and animation of pixel art.
the main objectives of the game were achieved satisfactorily. although the game itself was not fully completed, we would like to continue its development in the future.
