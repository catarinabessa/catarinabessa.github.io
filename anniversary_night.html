anniversary night is a pixel art videogame demo with an interactive narrative and includes multiple choices.

the game contains themes of homophobia and the pressure of being a cop, showing its consequences in one's life.

it was developed from march 2024 to june 2024.

the role of a police officer in games is often portrayed negatively and few cases in this medium explore the mental and physical consequences of being one on one's life.

but what if there was a game about an honest police officer?
what if he was dealing with family issues because of being a “hostage” to his job?
what would be the impact of a tragic night on a police officer's mind?
what if the police officer was part of the lgbtqia+ community?
how would that affect his life and his relationships with colleagues, both on and off duty?

all these questions, and many more, gave life to this project: an interactive narrative game about a gay police officer going through a hellish night with his husband and daughter. the player's choices will determine the protagonist's and his family's fate.
this project englobes homophobic themes in the workplace; being a hostage of their own job and how that affects their relationships; and the consequences that the dangerous job of being a policeman bring to the mind.

'until dawn', supermassive games, 2015

this game serves as the foundation for the gameplay of anniversary night. much of the gameplay revolves around multiple choices that shape the story's progression, as well as quick-time events scattered throughout the game, which can determine whether the character the player is controlling at the moment survives or dies.
to create a higher level of tension, anniversary night incorporate timers for choices and quick-time events that steer the story in completely different directions.
'undertale', toby fox, 2015

in undertale, each character has a unique sound—a pattern made up of “beeps” and “boops” that reflect their personality. whenever the player interacts with the inhabitants of the underground, these sounds can be heard, varying in rhythm, tone, and duration. this sound design choice was crucial in shaping the audio profile of the characters in anniversary night.

similar to bloomtown: a different story, anniversary night uses a black background to surround the environments, focusing the player's attention on the main action. additionally, anniversary night features speech bubbles above the speaking character instead of the typical text boxes that are very space consuming.

the minimalist design of the characters, represented by the use of few pixels and faceless designs, served as an important reference in developing the characters for anniversary night.
anniversary night is an interactive narrative game where the player assumes the role of paul, a gay police officer facing homophobia at work.

the game starts abruptly, immersing the player directly into a homophobic conversation between colleagues to enhance realism and empathy, avoiding formal introductions that might break immersion.
the player makes choices that impact paul's career, relationships, and the lives of his three family members.a timer is used during critical moments to add tension, forcing quick decisions that simulate real-life pressure, such as responding to life-or-death scenarios. as the story unfolds, the game explores the persistence of homophobia in modern police departments and its severe psychological consequences. by the end, the player witnesses how their decisions shaped the family's outcomes, encouraging replayability to reflect on and alter the story's course.

in total, 5 scripts in c# were created. these were the following: the main menu, the settings menu, the controller, the choices script and the text script.

the original sounds were all created on audacity while the rest, were reappropriations of sounds already existent that then suffered alterations.
the combination of realistic sounds and stylized dialogue creates an interesting auditory experience due to its contrast. on one hand, immersion and realism are present in the atmospheric sound design; on the other, the characters' “beeps” and “boops” add a more playful aspect to the audio experience. this enhances the gameplay and enriches the player's overall experience.
throughout the development of this project, we deepened our understanding of the topics we initially aimed to address, thanks to extensive research and investigation.
the real-life experiences of gay individuals helped us grasp the nature of discrimination, both past and present, in workplaces and daily life. we also came to better understand the various consequences of being a police officer, from the dangers and stress inherent to the profession to the impacts on mental health and the concerns for their loved ones.

this work provided a positive experience, fostering group unity and offering new learning opportunities for each member, ranging from programming to the creation and animation of pixel art.
the main objectives of the game were achieved satisfactorily. although the game itself was not fully completed, we would like to continue its development in the future.
